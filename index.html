Who I Am

⚡ Research Focus

My Masters-by-Research centers on evaluating commercial grounding enhancement materials — GEM, MEG, and SanEarth — under both low-voltage AC and high-voltage impulse conditions (100–300 kV). I've tested 13+ configurations controlling water content (0–10%), analyzed E₅₀, Zimp, and ionization thresholds, and modeled performance in SES-CDEGS. My work bridges lab characterization with real-world soil resistivity surveys and field grounding installations.

Publications

01
Performance of Grounding Enhancement Materials in Sandy Loam Soil Under Various Water Content Conditions for Lightning Protection
Investigates how three commercial grounding enhancement materials — GEM, MEG, and SanEarth — perform in sandy loam soil across varying water content levels (0–10%). The study evaluates DC resistance and impulse impedance to determine which material offers the most consistent protection under tropical soil conditions relevant to lightning protection design.
IEEE Access 2025 IEEE
→
02
Impulse Characteristics of Grounding Systems with Enhancement Materials Under High Voltage
Examines the high-voltage impulse behavior of grounding systems backfilled with enhancement materials, testing up to 300 kV. The paper analyzes impulse impedance (Zₖₚₚ), critical electric field (Eᾕ₀), and breakdown characteristics, revealing how EM-treated electrodes respond differently from untreated soil under fast-front lightning surges.
IEEE Xplore 2025 IEEE
→
03
AC and Impulse Characterization of Grounding Enhancement Materials with Controlled Water Content
Presents a dual-mode characterization of grounding enhancement materials using both low-voltage AC and high-voltage impulse testing across 13+ configurations with controlled water content from 0% to 10%. A calibrated DSO setup was used to capture precise impedance and ionization data, showing that AC and impulse performance trends do not always align.
Applied Sciences (MDPI) 2025 MDPI
→
04
Impulse Performance of Grounding Rods with Enhancement Materials in Sandy Loam Soil
Conference paper presenting field and lab results of vertical grounding rods installed in sandy loam soil treated with commercial enhancement materials. Tests were performed under both positive and negative impulse polarities, revealing polarity-dependent behavior and highlighting the inductive effect that becomes significant at low DC resistance values.
CPEEE 2025 2025 Conference

05
Comparison of Grounding Enhancement Materials for Low Resistivity Applications in Power Systems
A comparative study of GEM, MEG, and SanEarth in low-resistivity soil environments typical of tropical power system installations. The paper benchmarks DC resistance reduction, cost-effectiveness, and long-term stability of each material, providing practical guidance for engineers selecting backfill compounds for substation grounding.
CPEEE 2025 2025 Conference
→
06
Evaluation of Grounding Enhancement Material Performance Under AC Conditions in Tropical Soils
Evaluates the AC impedance characteristics of three enhancement materials mixed at different concentrations (5% WC, 5% WC + 10% EM, 10% WC + 10% EM) in tropical sandy loam soil. Using a calibrated parallel plate lab cell with a CT-based current measurement, the study maps impedance behavior across frequency and moisture variations relevant to Malaysia's climate.
Energy Engineering 2025 Journal
→
07
Low-Voltage AC Characterization of Grounding Enhancement Materials in Laboratory Soil Configurations
Reports LV-AC dry and wet testing of gravel and soil specimens mixed with GEM, MEG, and SanEarth under controlled water content from 0% to 10%. The paper introduces an air-gap AC test method to isolate material conductivity and establishes baseline impedance profiles for each EM type, offering a foundation for comparison with impulse results.
Applied Sciences (MDPI) 2025 MDPI
→
08
Influence of Water Content on the Grounding Resistance of Rods Backfilled with Enhancement Materials
Focuses on how varying water content levels from 0% to 10% affect the DC grounding resistance of vertical rod electrodes installed with enhancement material backfill. Results show that while EMs significantly reduce resistance, the improvement plateaus beyond a certain moisture threshold, with implications for maintenance scheduling in seasonal environments.
Bulletin of Electrical Engineering and Informatics 2025 Journal
→
09
Effect of Seasonal Soil Moisture on Grounding System Performance with Enhancement Materials
Investigates how natural seasonal fluctuations in soil moisture affect the long-term grounding performance of EM-treated electrodes. Using Fluke 1623-2 measurements at multiple sites across dry and wet seasons in Malaysia, the paper models resistance variation over time and proposes moisture-corrected grounding design guidelines.
Energies (MDPI) 2024 MDPI
→
10
Grounding Enhancement Material Performance at Varying Compaction and Moisture Levels
Studies how the degree of soil compaction around EM backfill interacts with moisture content to influence grounding resistance. Lab cell experiments with controlled compaction levels reveal that poorly compacted EM-soil mixtures lose up to 30% of their resistance-reduction benefit, stressing the importance of installation quality in the field.
Bulletin of Electrical Engineering and Informatics 2024 Journal
→
11
High-Voltage Impulse Testing of Grounding Rods with SanEarth and GEM Enhancement Materials
Conducts high-voltage impulse tests (100–300 kV) on rod electrodes treated with SanEarth and GEM, comparing their breakdown voltage (Eᾕ₀) and impulse impedance (Zᵢᵘᷬ) against untreated rods. The results show that both materials lower Zᵢᵘᷬ but exhibit distinct ionization onset levels, informing material selection for high-voltage protection applications.
IEEE Xplore 2024 IEEE
→
12
Polarity Effect on Impulse Characteristics of Grounding Systems with Enhancement Materials
Examines how impulse polarity (positive vs. negative) affects the ionization behavior, discharge time (tₐ), and impedance of EM-treated grounding electrodes. The study finds that while DC resistance is polarity-independent, the ionization onset and tail-end current behavior differ significantly, with implications for lightning protection design standards.
IEEE Xplore 2024 IEEE
→
13
Resistivity Reduction of Tropical Soils Using Commercial Grounding Enhancement Materials
Quantifies how GEM, MEG, and SanEarth reduce the bulk electrical resistivity of sandy loam and laterite soils common in Malaysia. Using four-probe Wenner measurements before and after EM application, the study demonstrates resistivity reductions of up to 60%, providing field-validated data for grounding engineers in tropical regions.
Bulletin of Electrical Engineering and Informatics 2024 Journal
→
14
Field Measurement and Modeling of Earth Resistance for Grounding Systems in Sandy Loam
Combines Fluke 1623-2 field resistivity measurements with SES-CDEGS multi-layer modeling to predict and validate DC earth resistance of grounding electrodes in sandy loam. The paper bridges the gap between simulated and measured grounding performance, offering a practical workflow for grounding system designers working in heterogeneous soil profiles.
Bulletin of Electrical Engineering and Informatics 2024 Journal
→
15
Impulse Characteristics of Soil Treated with Enhancement Materials and Various Moisture Contents
Presents field impulse tests (up to 100 kV) on a 2×2 m grid electrode installed in natural soil, EM-treated soil, and EM-treated soil with 10–100 L of added water. A notable finding is the inductive overshoot in voltage and current traces for low-resistance EM-treated electrodes — a phenomenon explained via PSPICE simulation showing the inductive effect becomes dominant when DC resistance is reduced.
Applied Sciences (MDPI) 2024 MDPI
→
16
AC Impedance Analysis of Grounding Enhancement Materials at Low Voltage in Sandy Loam Soil
Characterizes the AC impedance of GEM, MEG, and SanEarth embedded in sandy loam soil using a parallel-plate lab cell at low voltage. The paper maps frequency-dependent impedance profiles for each material, identifying resistive and capacitive components that influence power-frequency grounding performance and form the basis for further high-voltage testing.
Advances in Electrical and Electronic Engineering 2023 Journal
→
17
Experimental Study of Grounding Rod Performance with MEG Enhancement Material Under Impulse Conditions
Reports impulse test results for rod electrodes backfilled with MEG (Marconite Electrical Grade) under standard 1.2/50 µs lightning impulse waveforms. The study evaluates how MEG's carbon-graphite composition affects ionization threshold, impulse impedance, and current discharge time compared to unenhanced rods in the same soil environment.
IEEE Xplore 2023 IEEE
→
18
Characterization of Grounding Enhancement Material Mixtures in Controlled Laboratory Conditions
The earliest publication in this research series, establishing the laboratory methodology for characterizing grounding enhancement materials in controlled soil configurations. A custom-built lab rig with parallel plate electrodes was used to measure resistivity and AC impedance of GEM, MEG, and SanEarth at various mix ratios, laying the groundwork for all subsequent field and impulse studies.
IEEE Xplore 2022 IEEE
